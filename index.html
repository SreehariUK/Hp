Historical Places in India

Taj Mahal


The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, is one of the most iconic symbols of India. It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Hampi


Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Karnataka, famous for its ancient temples and ruins from the Vijayanagara Empire.

Jaipur City Palace


The City Palace in Jaipur is a stunning blend of Mughal and Rajput architecture, offering a glimpse into the royal heritage of Rajasthan.


Agra Fortt

The Agra Fort, located in Agra, is a UNESCO World Heritage site. It served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1638. The fort is a magnificent example of Mughal architecture, with its red sandstone walls enclosing numerous palaces, halls, and mosques.

Qutub Minar

The Qutb Minar, also spelled Qutub Minar and Qutab Minar, is a minaret and "victory tower" that forms part of the Qutb complex, which lies at the site of Delhi's oldest fortified city, Lal Kot, founded by the Tomar Rajputs.

Gateway of India

The Gateway of India is an arch-monument completed in 1924 on the waterfront of Mumbai (Bombay), India. It was erected to commemorate the landing of George V for his coronation as the Emperor of India in December 1911 at Strand Road near Wellington Fountain. He was the first British monarch to visit India.
